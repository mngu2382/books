Richard Flanagan: The Unknown Terrorist

October, 2014

Simply told and similar to Arthur & George in that narration/perspective is shared among the major characters.

Flanagan carries a moralizing tone throughout, very overbearing and slightly obnoxious, especially as I found it incongruous that he allows his characters to have these apprently philosophical views and yet have them carry on in their senselessly, almost shameless, shallow behaviour. Not to mention that I think Flanagan exaggerates societies excesses to make his point.
