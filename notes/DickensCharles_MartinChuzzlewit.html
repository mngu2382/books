Charles Dickens: Martin Chuzzlewit

September, 2014

Author/Narrator’s voice strong and vivacious, nearly always engaging.

A very long book, and I do tend to agree with the opinion that the American interlude was unnecessary.

Charismatic as the narrator is, I didn’t become engaged with the plot and characters until Pinch leaves Peckersniff. The change in Pinch from Peckersniff’s differential boarder to an assertive and outspoken chap on his visit to his sister was unexpected and quite marvellous, making me feel that Pinch, and not young Martin Chuzzlewit, is the leading protagonist.
